"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Furrow_irrigation"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Furrow irrigation"">furrow irrigation</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">A type of <a href=""#irrigation""><span title=""See entry on this page at § irrigation"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">irrigation</span></a> which relies on long, shallow, parallel channels, known as <i>furrows</i>, dug into the soil along the length of an agricultural field to deliver water to crops planted on the ridges between the furrows. Furrow irrigation is particularly suited to broadacre <a href=""#row_crop""><span title=""See entry on this page at § row crop"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">row crops</span></a> such as <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotton"" title=""Cotton"">cotton</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maize"" title=""Maize"">maize</a>, and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sugarcane"" title=""Sugarcane"">sugarcane</a>.</dd>

<dd class=""glossary"">Any collection of specific principles or methods applied by agricultural producers in order to create food or non-food products that are safe, healthy, and wholesome for consumers while also taking into account economic, social, and environmental <a href=""#sustainable_agriculture""><span title=""See entry on this page at § sustainable agriculture"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">sustainability</span></a>. "<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Growing_season"" title=""Growing season"">growing season</a>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">The part of the year during which local weather conditions (i.e. temperature and precipitation) permit the normal growth of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plant"" title=""Plant"">plants</a> in a given location. Though the timing of plant growth and reproduction can vary widely by species, many local plant species show considerable <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenology"" title=""Phenology"">phenological</a> overlap, and so the term is commonly used to refer to a single generic season that encompasses a majority of the plants or crops growing in a given location. In many places, the local ""growing season"" is defined as the period of time between the average date of the last <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frost"" title=""Frost"">frost</a> (in temperate parts of the <a href=""#northern_Hemisphere""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Northern Hemisphere"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Northern Hemisphere</span></a>, this typically occurs in the spring or early summer) and the average date of the first frost (typically in the autumn).</dd>
